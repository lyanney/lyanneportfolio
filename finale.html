Honey Bee vs. Wasp

Hover over body parts to see what they are!

Wasps have painful stingers that can sting multiple people, multiple times.
The wasp is full of hatred.
The hindlegs have a section called the pollen basket. The pollen basket collects pollen.
The forelegs are designed to clean the antennae.
The bee has two sets of eyes - compound and simple. These are the compound eyes. The compound eyes allow the bee to see in polarized vision.
This is where the reproductive organs of a bee are! On the underside of the abdomen are the wax glands. Worker bees use this to create wax.
Here are the wings! The wings can carry the bee through the air at 15 miles per hour!
This is the thorax! This is where the legs and wings attach. The honey bee has 3 pairs of legs.
This is the antennae. Each bee has two for sensing touch, smell, taste, and even hearing!
This is the stinger of the bee! Honey bees only sting for protection and when they sting, they die shortly afterward.
